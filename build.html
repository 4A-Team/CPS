Welcome to ProjectBIRD

We have developed a web and app-based experience that brings you up close to the birds of Mulligan's National Park in Australia. This unique soundscape is part nature retreat and part time machine, allowing you to experience a simulation of wildlife populations on any given day.

How does it work? Local park volunteers and staff collect photographs from stationary cameras placed in strategic nesting sites for birds recovering from the 2020 bushfires. Our machine learning tool sorts through those photos for three birds of interest: the Starling, the Magpie, and the Superb Parrot. As each bird is spotted, the time and date is placed into our sound archive. When you select a day below, you will experience sounds of each bird, collected by amateur volunteers, at the same time of day that any birds were observed on that day in history.

We hope this creates a relaxing ambient sound environment to help you work and study, with space and time to reflect on the recovery of nature through this unique experiential access point to bird tracking data. As bird populations recover, the soundscape will become more lively over time.

Artist Adaptions for ProjectBIRD

In this section we provide an overview of the tool for artists interested in blending machine learning and sound file generation. This is part user's guide and part documentation: written with the intention of being as clear as possible for those interested in using an image classification system to create audio soundscapes. For artists with access to a frequently updated, continuous stream of image data over larger time scales, this tool can translate that data into an archive of sound-triggers. This is most useful for the sonification of large, historic datasets with clear time stamps and dates. For this purpose, modification should be straightforward. For artists who are interested in real-time image capture and sound triggers, different systems may be better suited!
